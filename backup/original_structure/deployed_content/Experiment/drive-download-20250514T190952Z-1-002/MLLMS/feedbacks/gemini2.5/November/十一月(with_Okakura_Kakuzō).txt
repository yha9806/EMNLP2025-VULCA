While literati circles of the time may not have fully embraced this hybrid style, its impact on court aesthetics was profound and lasting. It represents a fascinating chapter in the history of Sino-Western artistic exchange, where the "rhythm of life" found expression through a brush that, while trained in Milan, learned to dance to the melodies of the Forbidden City. The work stands not only as a beautiful object but as a cultural document of immense significance.
