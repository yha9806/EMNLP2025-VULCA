**Style and Heritage:** This work is a prime example of the fusion style that Castiglione perfected and which became a defining characteristic of Qing court painting during its peak under Emperor Qianlong. While Castiglione adapted to the aesthetic preferences of the Chinese court, such as the use of even lighting and the emphasis on line, his European training remained foundational. This painting stands as a testament to the significant artistic and cultural exchange between China and Europe in the 18th century, showcasing how a foreign artist could not only adapt to but also significantly influence the artistic production of the imperial court. It builds upon the Chinese tradition of detailed narrative scrolls and court scenes while infusing them with a new sense of realism and spatial depth previously unseen.
